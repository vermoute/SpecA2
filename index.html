UNDERSTAND DIFFERENT PATHS

ABOUT

This project addresses Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 10: Reduced Inequalities by exploring how unequal opportunity shapes people’s life paths and outcomes. Drawing on Malcolm Gladwell’s argument that “These are stories, instead, about people who were given a special opportunity to work really hard… Their success was not just of their own making. It was a product of the world in which they grew up,” the project challenges the idea that success is determined solely by individual effort.

Using generative art developed with p5.js and JavaScript, the project transforms inequality of opportunity into an interactive system. By visualizing how different starting points and surrounding conditions shape unequal paths, it encourages viewers to recognize that success is not equally accessible to everyone. In doing so, the work aims to foster empathy and awareness of how opportunity is distributed unevenly across society.

Quote: “These are stories, instead, about people who were given a special opportunity to work really hard and seized it, and who happened to come of age at a time when that extraordinary effort was rewarded by the rest of society. Their success was not just of their own making. It was a product of the world in which they grew up.”

Author: Malcolm Gladwell

Publication: Outliers - The Story of Success

Michael J. Sandel’s statement highlights how outcomes are shaped not only by individual effort, but also by the conditions people are given — particularly the starting points and environments that shape their access to opportunity.
